The Proposal

How we think it happened...

Sarah Pearson

I'll try and keep this gag worthy story to a minimum, so bear with me. So upon arrival in Amsterdam, we knew there was a 9 hour layover waiting for us, and had considered the possibility of heading into the city for a few hours to explore. Now me being the daughter of Solveig, I was a little skeptical of the whole going in and out of the country so quickly thing, but after talking to a KLM worker who suggested it, and Caleb very pointedly telling me we should do this, I agreed. One stamp in the passport and a cafe latte later, I started walking toward the train ticket dispenser, only to receive a tug on my arm from Caleb and a grinning face that said “Oh I have something else planned for us today..” Excuse me? Why did you not feel the need to inform me of this change of plans? NOT ON THE ITINERARY.

A half hour later after standing in a line for something I did not know what it was, we boarded the a bus to take us to… the Keukenhof Flower Preserve! If you look it up, you will realize you've seen pictures of it before. I've always wanted to go, whether Caleb knew that or not is unknown, but we'll give him this one. After arriving with a throng of other tourists, we began the slow, beautiful, lovely smelling walk around the GIGANTIC grounds. Man is that place full of some well gardened flowers, all I could think of is that this is where Carol (Caleb's momma) should retire and work one day. Just a thought :) Around this time Caleb started getting very fidgety and agitated, which usually means he needs to be fed, and quickly. So after a delicious gigantic bratwurst, we set out to find these delicious Dutch Maple Waffles that I'm obsessed with, or so I thought. At one point he meandered over to the side on the grass and told me he wanted to put his camera away, and bent down to open up the slot in his backpack, handing me his camera to help. Well lo and behold I look down to hand it to him and there he is, one knee and all asking me to marry him.

Caleb Harrison

Let's skip past all the logistics, and start on the good ol Amsterdam day. I've got a ring in a box, rolled up in a raincoat tucked into the bottom of my bag. And I have two bus tickets/entrance passes for this Keukenhof business that everyone seems to be so fond of. And so I spend alllll the time before the trip convincing Sarah that 9 hours is entirely too short of a layover to really go out and do anything: “welll, the last time I was there it was a bit troublesome going in and out” and “I mean once you factor in transportation and security, there's not much time to really do anything..” Lies, all of them. But if she started thinking of things to do, she'd find something cooler and that was not going to happen.

So we listen to the kind KLM peoples' advice, and head for the exits. Sarah goes for the train tickets – denied. We are going elsewhere. After a RIDICULOUSLY HOT AND SWEATY bus ride (the sun was just blasting in through the window, it was sweaty and gross I tell you), we arrive at Keukenhof. The place is a garden paradise, and I am pretty ambivalent towards these kinds of things. It smelled great (yes, even I could smell them), and there were pretties everywhere. So we walked around and I haphazardly took pictures without being able to really pay attention to much of anything (slightly distracted as I was). Eventually I just gave up taking pictures, and was scouting out locations to drop the M-Bomb. Nice little daffodil garden by the creek. Filled with Japanese tourists. Nice white-flowered tree in a nook. Filled with an overweight lady with short hair, taking flower pics with her dog. A water fountain framed with hedges and sculptures. Hipster French kids complaining in both English and French about The Establishment. (Newsflash-that-isn't-news: everyone in Europe speaks five languages fluently and makes me feel like a dum-dum) You get the point, nothing was really working. So I sporadically dragged Sarah around, trying to feign interest in some sort of colored plant or another to check out locales.

After getting perhaps a tidge fussy, and having stomach things begin to occur, we grabbed a delicious brat and smoothie (which probably cost like $30 for two pairs of each) and sat down. Knowing she wanted her waffles, and that my stomach wouldn't last much longer, I figured I'd just find a place to do it before we got to the waffles. I had visited the bathroom at least twice already while in the park, moving the ring box first to the top of my backpack, then to the camera pouch. Finally, after meandering in a helter-skelter path roughly back to the waffles, I figured this spot was as good as any, and (probably awkwardly) just stopped us and mumbled some excuse. I got down, did the thing, and Sarah thankfully didn't say no (though she did say "Really?? Are you serious?" a few times, which was cute).

Anyway, there it is. I don't know if either of us actually knows what was said by the other (or ourselves). But we were both instantly pretty tired and ready to go back to the airport and sleep.
